SFS@BAMA Course Requirements

Course Requirements Within Major



Scholars must complete at least 3 courses from the following list



Computer Science Criminology and Criminal Justice Management Information Systems


Cyber Security students have no additional requirements.


Computer Science students must complete the Cyber Security Concentration.		 CC201 - Introduction to Cyber Criminology (FA/SP) MIS421 - Enterprise Application Development
CC290 - Global Cyber Threats (FA) MIS460 - Applied Cybersecurity
CC301 - Cyber Law and Policy (SP) MIS462 - Behavioral Cybersecurity
CC395 - JECTF Internship (FA/SP) MIS464 - Organizational Security Management
CC401 - Law Enforcement in the Digital Age (FA) MIS466 - Introduction to Cybercrime & Digital Forensics
CC402 - Digital Forensic Investigation (SP)
CC403 - Blockchain, Cryptocurrencies, and Criminal Investigations (SP)

Course Requirements Outside Major



Scholars must complete at least 9 hours outside their home department. These hours are chosen from the lists below and must include at least 3 hours taken from each non-home department.



Computer Science Criminology and Criminal Justice Management Information Systems*
CS104 - Computer Science Principles (FA/SP) CC201 - Introduction to Cyber Criminology (FA/SP) MIS460 - Applied Cyber Security (FA)
CS340 - Legal & Ethical Issues in Computing (FA/SP) CC301 - Cyber Law and Policy (SP) MIS462 - Behavioral Cyber Security (FA)
CS345 - Advanced Legal & Ethical Issues in Computing (FA/SP) CC401 - Law Enforcement in the Digital Age (FA) MIS464 - Organizational Security Management (SP)
CC402 - Digital Forensics Investigation (SP)
CC403 - Blockchain, Cryptocurrencies, and Criminal Investigations (SP)
*Prerequisites for these courses will be waived for non-MIS SFS@BAMA students. Students must request overrides through the MIS Academic Advisor, Heather Davis.
